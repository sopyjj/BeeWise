How to Spot a Varroa Mite

Before you start counting, learn what to look for. Study 5 real bee images — then see the expert's answer with the mite highlighted.

  • 1 Varroa mites are reddish-brown and oval/circular — like a small rust-coloured button attached to the bee.
  • 2 They attach to the bee's abdomen (the striped rear section). Look for a round, dark-reddish speck that doesn't match the bee's natural pattern.
  • 3 Mites are about 1–1.8 mm wide — roughly a sesame seed — so they stand out as a distinct shape against the bee's body.
  • 4 Click where you think the mite is, then hit See Answer to reveal the correct location with a red circle.
