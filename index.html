About BeeWise

BeeWise was born in 2024 from a simple yet profound question — “How can AI heal our planet?” What began as a student-led exploration into honeybee decline grew into an AI-driven ecological project, combining computer vision, empathy, and environmental science.

Early experiments using YOLO object detection models proved that technology could identify Varroa mites on honeybees in real time. This breakthrough led to BeCure — a mobile hive-monitoring app recognized globally at the Oxford Climate Change Challenge (COP29) for merging innovation with environmental ethics.

Building Ecological Intelligence

In 2025, BeeWise grew into a youth-led social venture that integrates artificial intelligence, ecology, and education. Its mission is to cultivate Ecological Intelligence — the ability for humans and machines to learn coexistence through empathy and innovation.

Through initiatives like “To Bee Friends”, the team reached over 200 children with workshops combining storytelling, seedball-making, and art. Collaborating with Korea Beekeepers Association and Seoul National University’s InValab, BeeWise now works toward building a global youth eco-tech network to inspire the next generation of sustainability leaders.

Our Journey

BeeWise Youth Social Venture History

From a student idea to an internationally recognized youth platform merging AI, ecology, and ethics.

June – August 2024

Origins of BeeWise

We began with a question: “How can AI heal our planet?”

During the summer, we studied global honeybee decline and explored how computer vision could help monitor hive health. With YOLO-based experiments and guidance from Korean beekeepers and environmental NGOs, we saw how scientific inquiry can evolve into real-world impact.

September – December 2024

BeCure App Development

We developed BeCure, an AI-powered hive monitoring app that detects Varroa mites with a smartphone camera and alerts beekeepers in real time.

Collaborating with AI mentors and student developers, we trained YOLO models and combined technical innovation with environmental ethics—earning international attention.

As a result, we presented at the COP29 youth environment section in Baku, Azerbaijan, and won Runner-Up at the Oxford Climate Change Challenge among 208 teams from 53 countries.

January – March 2025

Establishing BeeWise

Building on BeCure’s success, we formally founded BeeWise, a youth-led social venture uniting artificial intelligence, ecology, and education.

Our aim goes beyond problem-solving: we cultivate Ecological Intelligence—the capacity for humans and machines to learn coexistence through empathy and innovation.

April – June 2025

To Bee Friends Environmental Education Program

We believe environmental awareness starts with empathy in early childhood. “To Bee Friends” helps children see bees not as something to fear, but as living beings to protect and respect.

Designed entirely by the BeeWise team, the program blends environmental storytelling, melliferous seedball-making, and upcycled art—connecting scientific understanding with emotional experience.

Working with the Korea Beekeepers Association and the Korean Youth Beekeepers Association, we incorporated ecological consultation and field feedback. The program was selected Top 5 in the Gyeonggi Volunthon (₩1,000,000 grant) and advanced with Seoul National University’s InValab.

July – September 2025

Implementation and Impact

We implemented the curriculum in 25+ kindergartens across Suwon, engaging over 200 students through creative, hands-on workshops.

We also launched an online TBF learning app and distributed 800 seedball kits to broaden access—earning the 2025 Gyeonggi Province Volunteer Grand Prize.

October 2025 & Beyond

Global Partnership and Vision

We are building a youth-centered environmental & technology network linking Asia and the United States, partnering with international NGOs and universities to scale our AI-Edu ecological education platform globally.

We have initiated a youth bee-conservation partnership with Frosch and are preparing collaborations with UNFCCC/YOUNGO to contribute to global climate and innovation dialogue.

BeeWise aims to nurture a generation that heals the planet with technology—elevating AI from a tool to a language of coexistence and responsibility.

Meet Our Team

The BeeWise Team

United by a shared mission to merge technology, ecology, and empathy, our founders lead BeeWise in transforming environmental innovation through data, design, and education.

Grace Kim

Co-founder
Director of Environmental Humanities & Data Analysis

Phillips Academy Andover

Grace leads the humanistic, analytical, and narrative vision of BeeWise. As a digital storyteller, she integrates ecological ethics with data-driven research—translating complex environmental data into compelling narratives that inspire understanding and empathy. Her work bridges sustainability, philosophy, and computational analysis, revealing how technology can mirror human responsibility toward nature. Through her leadership, BeeWise initiatives remain both scientifically rigorous and ethically grounded—turning data into stories that move people to act for the planet.

Sophia Kim

Co-founder
Director of Environmental Arts & Technology

Phillips Academy Andover

Sophia drives the creative and technological development of BeeWise, leading the design of BeeCure 2.0 and the Edge Box smart hive monitoring system. As a digital creator, she merges artificial intelligence, visual storytelling, and design thinking to craft immersive learning tools that connect youth to sustainability through creativity and innovation. Her approach transforms environmental education into a dynamic, tech-powered experience—redefining how the next generation learns, feels, and designs for ecological coexistence.

Edward Yoon

Co-founder
Director of Environmental Technology & Environmental Justice

Seoul International School

Edward leads the technological and scientific research division of BeeWise, focusing on the intersection of artificial intelligence, biodiversity, and agricultural resilience. His work explores seed sovereignty—the right of communities to preserve and control local melliferous plant species essential to pollinator survival—linking environmental data science with grassroots ecological justice. Through AI-powered seed mapping and sustainability modeling, he investigates how technology can empower local ecosystems rather than exploit them. Edward’s research embodies a new paradigm of environmental justice, where innovation serves regeneration, and data becomes a tool for restoring both ecological and cultural balance.
